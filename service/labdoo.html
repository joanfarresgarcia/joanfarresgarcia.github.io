Labdoo PROJECT

From january to may, 2018, a students including myself continued the organization of a system where we would restore old computers and install the Ubuntu operating system onto them so they could be sent to other students in need of them. We met five intense days (January 17th 2018, January 31st 2018, February 13rd 2018, April 10th 2018 and May 2nd 2018) to restore the computers. We quickly realized that the most important aspect of the project was organization and communication.

First of all, we quickly divided ourselfs into groups. One of the students took care of documentation before sending the computers. Although it was an easy step, it was time-taking and having a member dedicated to doing that was much better than having everyone do it, as it requiered some specific information on the destination, the computer's battery size, the serial number. Teaching everyone would have been chaotic.

The rest of the students would take a computer and plug in a USB to install the needed Operating System (OS). Since we also needed the charger for every computer, which often would accept weird adapters, we would often have a couple of members near them that would helf them find the adapter according to the computer, although not always.

Within the students that would install the OS, we were often separated into students that would load the OS and manage the BIOS system, and those that would finish the installation. That proved very efficient, as many different computers had different architectures and BIOS systems, which made it hard for students not as familiar with the BIOS interface to install them. For instance, I was one of the members that loaded Ubuntu into the computers as I was very familiar with operating systems and I had install many. As a consequence, I often found myself helping others.

However, sometimes some of us would get distracted, which would result in a disproportion between loaded OSs and installed ones. Another problem we encountered was that computers would take a long time to install the OS, which meant that we had couldn't complete installation that very seesion. As a result, some students would return the following day to finish it.

To conclude, we were very glad we were able to use our knowledge of computers to help other students in other places in the world, and in the process, improve drastically our project-management and communication. We all learned a lot in the process, especially regarding older computer architectures and BIOS systems.
